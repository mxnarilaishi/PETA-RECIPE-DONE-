Shrimp Sinigang

A guide for cooking this sour delicous shrimp sinigang.

20-30 minutes cooking time

Serves 4–6

INGREDIENTS

  • Shrimp 500g medium shrimp, cleaned and deveined (heads and shells can be left on for flavor)
  • Tamarind mix 1 pack (20g) Knorr Sinigang sa Sampalok Mix Original or fresh tamarind pulp
  • Rice washing 4 cups (or plain water)
  • Onion 1 medium, quartered
  • Tomatoes 2–3 medium, quartered
  • Radish ½ piece, sliced
  • >Eggplant 1 medium, sliced
  • Okra 3–6 pieces, trimmed
  • String beans (sitaw) 4 pieces, cut into 3-inch lengths
  • Water spinach (kangkong) 1 bunch, stalks and leaves separated
  • Green chili 1 piece, whole
  • Fish sauce to taste
  • Salt optional, for adjusting flavor

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Prepare the broth
    Pour 4 cups rice washing (or water) into a large pot. Add onion and tomatoes. Bring to a gentle boil for about 8 minutes until tomatoes soften.
  2. Add shrimp and vegetables
    Add shrimp, green chili, radish, eggplant, and okra. Simmer until shrimp turn pink-orange and vegetables are tender (5–7 minutes).
  3. Add string beans and kangkong stalks
    Cook for another 2 minutes until slightly tender.
  4. Flavor with tamarind mix
    Stir in the tamarind mix until dissolved. Taste the broth and adjust with fish sauce or salt.
  5. Finish with kangkong leaves
    Add kangkong leaves last, letting them wilt for about 1 minute.
  6. Serve hot
    Ladle into bowls and serve with steamed rice. Optional: patis (fish sauce) with calamansi on the side for dipping.

About

Shrimp Sinigang (Sinigang na Hipon) is a classic Filipino sour soup made with fresh shrimp, tamarind broth, and assorted vegetables.
